Overview

Dolby Digital Plus, formally known as Enhanced AC-3 (E-AC-3), is an advanced lossy
audio compression technology that extends the capabilities of the original AC-3 codec.
Introduced in 2005 and standardized as ETSI TS 102 366, E-AC-3 provides improved
compression efficiency, higher bit rates, and support for up to 15.1 discrete channels,
making it suitable for modern streaming applications and Blu-ray discs.

E-AC-3 maintains backward compatibility with AC-3 decoders while offering approximately
50% better compression efficiency at comparable quality levels. This efficiency makes it
ideal for streaming services where bandwidth is limited, operating effectively at
128-256 kbps for 5.1 audio compared to AC-3's typical 384-448 kbps. The codec supports
higher sampling rates (up to 48 kHz), more channels, and improved spectral resolution,
while serving as a transport format for Dolby Atmos object-based audio metadata.

Key Features

- Extended channel support: Up to 15.1 discrete channels (vs AC-3's 5.1)
- Improved efficiency: ~50% better compression than AC-3 at equivalent quality
- Higher maximum bit rate: Up to 6 Mbps (vs AC-3's 640 kbps)
- Backward compatible: Can decode to AC-3 for legacy devices
- Spectral extension: Improved high-frequency reproduction
- Enhanced coupling: More efficient channel coupling algorithms
- Transient pre-noise processing: Better handling of sharp transients
- Multiple independent substreams: Up to 8 programs in one stream
- Dolby Atmos container: Carries object-based audio metadata
- Frame sizes: 256, 512, 768, or 1536 samples for flexibility

Encoding:
- Transform: Enhanced MDCT with improved spectral resolution
- Block sizes: 256, 512, 768, 1536 samples (adaptive)
- Frame size: 256-6144 samples (flexible framing)
- Coupling: Advanced frequency coupling for channel correlation
- Spectral extension: Bandwidth replication techniques
- Vector quantization: Improved coefficient coding

Bit Rates:
- 5.1 channels: 128-768 kbps (typical streaming: 192-256 kbps)
- 7.1 channels: 256-1024 kbps
- Stereo: 64-192 kbps
- Maximum: 6.144 Mbps

Sampling Rates:
- 32 kHz, 44.1 kHz, 48 kHz (standard)
- Supports half rates: 16 kHz, 22.05 kHz, 24 kHz

Channel Configurations:
- All AC-3 configurations (1.0 to 5.1)
- 6.1, 7.1 (rear/side surrounds)
- Up to 13.1 bed channels + 2 extension channels

File Extensions:
- .ec3 (raw E-AC-3 elementary stream)
- .eac3
- Typically muxed in .mp4, .m4a, .ts, .mkv containers

Important Notes

- Licensing required through Dolby Laboratories for implementation
- Not all devices support E-AC-3; AC-3 fallback often provided

- Can carry Dolby Atmos metadata without requiring discrete object channels
