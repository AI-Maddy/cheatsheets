RS-422
======

**Domain:** Embedded/Avionics/Industrial

**Description:**
RS-422 is a differential, point-to-point serial communication standard, widely used for long-distance, high-speed data transfer in industrial, embedded, and avionics systems.

**Key Features:**
- 📡 Differential, point-to-point
- 🛡️ Long distance, high speed
- 🧩 Simple, robust
- 🔄 Multi-drop (one driver, up to 10 receivers)

**Physical/Electrical Layer:**
- 🔌 Differential signaling, twisted pair
- ⚡ Data rates: up to 10 Mbps
- 📏 Distance: up to 1,200 meters

**Topology:**
- 🌐 Point-to-point, multi-drop

**Use Cases:**
- 🖥️ Industrial automation, PLCs
- ✈️ Avionics, sensors, displays
- 🛠️ Embedded systems

**Pros:**
- 🧩 Simple, reliable, long range
- 🚀 Higher speed than RS-232

**Cons:**
- 🏗️ One driver per bus
- ⚠️ Not as flexible as RS-485

**Standard:**
- 📜 EIA/TIA-422

**Typical Applications:**
- 🏭 Factories, aircraft, embedded devices

**Memorization Tip:**
<span style="color:#1976d2;">Think of RS-422 as the "long wire for fast data"—simple, tough, and perfect for long runs!</span>
