XCP
===

**Domain:** Automotive

**Description:**
Calibration protocol for automotive ECUs.

**Key Features:**
- 🛠️ Standard for ECU measurement/calibration
- 🔄 Runs over CAN, Ethernet, FlexRay, USB
- 🧩 Supports data acquisition, flashing
- 🛡️ Real-time, high-speed

**Physical/Electrical Layer:**
- 🔌 CAN, Ethernet, FlexRay, USB
- ⚡ Data rates: up to 1 Gbps (Ethernet)

**Topology:**
- 🌟 Point-to-point, star

**Use Cases:**
- 🚗 ECU calibration, flashing, diagnostics
- 🛠️ Automotive development, testing

**Pros:**
- 🚀 Fast, flexible, multi-bus
- 🧩 Standardized, widely supported

**Cons:**
- 🏗️ Requires compatible tools
- ⚠️ Security considerations for flashing

**Standard:**
- 📜 ASAM MCD-1 XCP

**Typical Applications:**
- 🚗 Automotive R&D, ECU development

**Memorization Tip:**
<span style="color:#1976d2;">Think of XCP as the "mechanic's remote control"—tune, test, and update your car's brain from your laptop!</span>
