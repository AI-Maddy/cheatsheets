FlexRay
=======

**Domain:** Automotive

**Description:**
Deterministic, high-speed, fault-tolerant bus (used in chassis/safety systems like steer-by-wire; still present but less dominant now).

**Key Features:**
- ⚡ Up to 10 Mbps data rate
- 🔄 Time- and event-triggered communication
- 🛡️ Fault-tolerant, redundant channels
- 🧩 Supports up to 64 nodes

**Physical/Electrical Layer:**
- 🔌 Twisted pair cabling
- ⚡ Data rates: up to 10 Mbps

**Topology:**
- 🌟 Star, bus, or hybrid

**Use Cases:**
- 🚗 Steer-by-wire, brake-by-wire, safety systems
- 🛠️ Chassis, powertrain, ADAS

**Pros:**
- 🚀 High speed, deterministic
- 🛡️ Fault-tolerant, reliable

**Cons:**
- 🏗️ More complex and costly than CAN
- ⚠️ Less common in new designs

**Standard:**
- 📜 ISO 17458

**Typical Applications:**
- 🚗 Premium vehicles, safety-critical systems

**Memorization Tip:**
<span style="color:#1976d2;">Think of FlexRay as the "racecar pit crew"—fast, reliable, and always ready for action!</span>
